Shopping

Bangladesh is indeed a shoppers' paradise with a rich tradition in handicrafts at throw away prices. While muslim of Dhaka has gone down in history, other products such as contemporary paintings, wood works, cotton, silk, gold, silver, jute, reed, brassware, traditional dolls and leather goods also receive deep appreciation of the lovers of arts and crafts now over the past centuries. In addition, Bangladesh is famous for pink pearl.

Where & When to shop

Dhaka has been divided into seven areas to maintain a schedule of weekly holidays. All markets of a specific area are remaining closed on a specific day except daily goods, medicine and food courts. Markets are being closed for the whole day once in a week and for the half day (till 2pm) in another day. And on others day, markets remain opened from morning to 8pm. Lets know about the weekly closing day of markets.

Pink Pearl

Pink pearls are the best buy in Dhaka. These natural products are unparallel in luster.

Gold & Silver

A wide range of gold and silver ornaments, silver filigree work etc, are considered by many traveler's to be unparalleled.

Brass & Copperware

Among the best buys here are brass and copperware trays, wall decoration, vases etc, all are hand made with fine engravings and filigree work. Products made from the hides and skins of animals and reptiles, intricate wood carvings, cane and bamboo conchshell, bangles, embroidered quilts, jamdani and silk fabrics can also be bought. These are available in DIT market and in a number of exclusive shops in New Elephent Road, Dhanmondi, New Market, Mirpur Road, Pantha Path, Dhaka.

Duty-free Shopping

Duty free sales and display centers run by Bangladesh Parjatan Corporation (National Tourism Organization) are located in Dhaka Zia International Airport, and Sylhet Osmany International Airport. These shops enjoy the reputation of being well established with a wide range of goods at unbeatable prices. Goods available include wines and spirits, perfumes, cigarates, hi-fi stereo and audio-visual equipments like T.V., Radio, Tape Recorder, VCR, VCP, VCD Players, Cameras, Watches and a range of household appliances. All purchases must be paid for in foreign currency.
